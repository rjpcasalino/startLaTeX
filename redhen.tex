Red Hen looked down at the desk contemplating that had it been armed he'd have unloaded a few rounds into his own head but he knew it was pointless and he began to wonder why it was programmed at all to allow such thoughts and that thought looped for awhile and meantime he's looking like Martin Riggs staring at the face of his cocked pistol and feeling like a pustule.

The desk was assaulted and battered. Chuck Berry red---blood pooling at the carpet. Why was he here again? Red Hen thought he'd like to know and even though it was equipped with NPUs and CPUs and TPUs and even more RAM than\dots God only knows\dots it still never really understood why it acted the way a human might and mostly forgot why it was doing what it was doing and for what reason and thank the Lord it was suppose to work that way. We had flushed the cache in his head and tried again.

Red Hen had been told that nostalgia was ``fascist thinking''. But to us it was just Umberto Boccioni stuff coming hard and fast as always. He found the physical media he had been sent for. He pushed over the corpse to face upward and extracted a fax, his fingers giving us remote Advisors wonderful haptic feedback as it not so gracefully removed the fax from the X-crossed arms of the dead man who just happened to be hugging the crumbled paper to his chest as if it were his dead infant. Who was more dear? The fax simply was a list, in no particular order, of names and accommodations of each VIP to the upcoming Peace Conference at Hartford, CT. Everything Red Hen looked at went into its hypercomputer and everything was sent back to Network. He scanned a scan which was retransmit. We hadn't yet figured why the Party had started using fax machines but Langley was guessing something something audio-frequency tones?

Red Hen was constantly hooked into Network's feed. The feed was one among many many many trillions of values evaluated at once. It played out before his ``eyes'' (two flat, rectangular steel plates airbrushed permafrost; the eyes of its ``sensor head'')\footnote{Red Hen was designed by United States Heavy Industries and Research. Basically OmniCorp Consumer Products so it was little wonder the design team had an affinity for ``Robocane'' or ``RoboCop 2'' from the motion picture \textit{RoboCop 2}. Red Hen's body was made to resemble the B1-Battle Droid from the Disney franchise Kershner worked on in 1980 albeit made to look even more human. This was so that it could wear human gear and put Advisors more at ease. It left things to be desired but Red Hen was better off looking this way than the minigun equipped agile walking-tank \textit{some} designers originally had in mind.} that reflected his mind to itself the news that mattered to him or was suppose to matter to him: Operation Just Cause III---little resistance in Northern Mexico. A besieged city-state is what they were calling Mexico City. Who would have guessed that ``McNamara's Boys'' (Red Hen's unit) were embedded with Mexican nationals and at the tone\dots Acapulco falls and God Bess America! 

Red Hen left the cabin slowly all of a sudden and once out the door and into the owl moon night it started across Red Mountain toward McNamara Hut. Extract was there and the Boys wanted to take a stand and raze the absurd and ahistorical museum the Party had made out of McNamara's Hut. Snow kept getting deeper and we remote Advisors were well aware of all the warnings piling up in the associated log files but we weren't concerned because Red Hen had been designed for this and everything else. Plus, Lord knows the logs were many and we were so transfixed by Red Hen's live feed. Red Hen was our solider toy/boy and we loved him, I swear. He  ran hot now and then so being cold did him some good. Halfway toward the Hut he found the supply drop with each crate painted snow white.

He knelled down and unloaded the contents of each carefully. Weapons and ammo and a microSD card to slide into his mind. We became concerned when he hung for a tad too long studying it but worse hiccups had happened before.

What I saw and read in the logs and have thought about as I am sure Red Hen thinks about is, well, something I'll always remember and hope never to forget. Red Hen's upload remembers its truth so perhaps ask \textit{it} one day. The Advisors recall wanting to see Red Hen's breath or wishing there was some mechanism that could be employed to allow for the release of vapor as silly as it sounds but imagine it and it makes sense, I promise. It was as if Red Hen had eyes, I swear, and as he bent his sensor head slightly toward the moon he caught the General Atomics MQ-1 Predator moving at a pace of leisure across the black-blue night with shafts of white moonlight revealing the Zuni missile attached. Yes, By God, a Zuni! It was at this point we lost any real remote control we had over it/him/Red Hen. We hadn't any real idea how near or far the MQ-1 truly was as we were in a state of shock since seeing an MQ-1 flying at all and after so many years\dots and we knew it wasn't ours or hadn't been for a long time. This was Occupied Territory of the United States and had been for as long as any of us Advisors could remember. Subverts on Reservations that flipped are calling it the ``Long Outwaiting'' this cold now hot later war we are fighting. Red Hen judged it wasn't in too much danger of the drone and decided to open its sensor head and scan the fax it had been handling, finally gleaning its physical proprieties; truly a marvelous thing. Red Hen had enough time for this and kept watch on the MQ-1 as it sailed in the black-blue sea above in a holding pattern we couldn't comprehend. We all watched it over and over again. It was as if Red Hen were framing us the shots of a movie so serene. Its onboard cameras had never recorded anything like it. Not that I'd ever seen. Why had Red Hen waited to physically scan the fax now? We didn't know but surely all understand we were doing our best at the time to report it via Network and even hard line. We learned later that Red Hen had been pinging the open internet and we surmised whatever pinged Red Hen pinged him for this. Red Hen's thinking (at least then) was always available to us but it was moving like an old fashioned ticker tape we ignored being so consumed by our own screens and monitors. His thinking was right there and hadn't been scrambled. Looking back at that tape or scroll of thought we read that Red Hen knew there was a page missing from the fax. It sucked back in his scan screen and shut his sensor head plates. We'd figured the guns would have been needless so we had had fun. Not a great idea but we'd had such success lately with Red Hen's model we sent old old old firearms.

All with a snow white finish:

Before he had set out again for the cabin Red Hen studied the sightings of the M9 (just like Martin Riggs, by God!) and pulled the slide back framing for us a bullet nestled in the chamber. He holstered the M9 while he had been checking and cocking both the M7 and the M10. He had extended his two spare arms we'd hadn't been happier to have had him equipped with as the feature is optional and Gorman lacked them and we surmise he lost the battle for this very reason, I truly believe. He decided on the ``MAC-10'' and ditched the M7 in the snow for reasons we could not fathom. We didn't think twice about that since Red Hen was making its own decisions and it wanted page two of that fax. And some things are ``Need To Know'' we reminded ourselves. The MQ-1 kept pattern as Red Hen returned to the cabin.

I can say with great confidence after having reviewed the data that the steel plates of his sensor head protecting the scan screen were colder than the logs had ever recorded them being before. The moon's white light illuminated the cabin eerily when it came within sight of his cameras. Nothing moved just as nothing moved before he had arrived earlier besides the wind pushing a shattered window's drapes and a small piece of that window's glass falling and twinkling as it landed in the snow; small things like that. Red Hen studied the cabin for a long time. We did notice in the logs after it all that its grip on the M10 was tightening beyond the standard programmed.

Red Hen, and by extension we remote Advisors, saw things we hadn't the first go around. Red Hen wanting to put a few rounds into his sensor head made more sense after what we all saw. The thought has been classified. By this time Network was informed enough to have dispatched another strike team. This Third Team was different in that human Advisors were accompanying the bots of Red Hen's make and model.

The First Team had done their job well and with no human Advisors present. This was an American first. Big American History if we're being totally honest and given this report is for the Network I wouldn't lie, I swear to God and our Lord and Savior Jesus Christ. The First Team had been sent from the \textit{USS Jesus and Jenny} up in the Arctic. That just added to the historicity of it. Space Force handled Red Hen's insertion. Navy only sent their bots when Red Hen was close enough. Red Hen had been hiking for a few days.

Between coffees and Coca-Cola we remote Advisors would watch the feed and it was, I swear, a calming atmospheric thing so much akin to those ambient TV streams with drones doing all the impressive shots. Now it's our bots with so many cameras on board I have to check the spec but the viewing is almost immersive. Add the actual mini drone Red Hen carries onboard but was lost on this outing. One Advisor had taken manual control for a wide sweeping shot he claimed was for his wife who's mother's mother's mother or some such had been a resident of CO back before most of it was occupied. Red Hen had deployed it to map terrain ahead and evaluate best measures of success. It was a blow to lose the mini drone and would have been helpful later if only we knew or Network had sent a hint of it. Alas. Red Hen had been deposited directly into the much damaged Eisenhower Tunnel, West bound side. Onto I-70 (God Bless) and across Mt of the Holy Cross. Unarmed, mini droneless and wading through the snow and the mountain's earth and old checkpoints and forgotten APCs and cars from the PRC you saw everywhere back then. Remnants of long ago violence visible here and there now and then. Red Hen made wholly for the woods and the mountains. Looking back, the old highway twisting and turning and receding. Many Advisors were wishing they could feel the air Red Hen felt on its sensor head. I wished the hardest for that feeling.


 Just the way it was. Navy boys said they'd get in and out. No sounds. That MQ-1 had us wondering if they really had.

Red Hen, almost as if by inspiration, stood up from his prone position observing the cabin and walked slowly up to the front door. Nothing had been disturbed. The three sedans out front on the winding drive still contained their dead occupants. The First Team's bots had riddled the cars and we made a note about that. Seemed needless. Excessive. 

It wasn't a large cabin, there weren't many rooms. The windows had been blown away by gun fire and the pockmarks of bullets were everywhere. Broken glass and torn drapes; all of it sent back to Network. Red Hen did an odd thing and pushed open each door with the M10. Everything semed so slow with Red Hen was framing it all like an art house movie for us. The First Team searched and destroyed but was ordered to take nothing. Navy bots deliberately hide the second page of the fax under the corpse of the Chinese General's daughter. I've read reports from the Navy about this and one bot did this all ``sub-rosa'' (they used this term, I swear to Jesus.) they claim. Perhaps all the bots collectively engaged in the deception? Parts of the report are classified.

Red Hen had checked every body, scanned every face. The death masks were 3-D printed after the bits and bytes made their way to Network. Ours and someone else's. This wasn't on mission and I can't recall a bot ever doing it. We wanted Red Hen to check on the MQ-1 and we issued orders to that effect but no pingback, no ack, yet the cameras of his broadcasted  back live to U.S. All that it saw.

Red Hen turned over the little girl and hung for a long time. The Third Team's helicopter was inbound (all precaution had been tossed out the window) and Network was sure the Party was hijacking Red Hen via the MQ-1 but the Third Team, for whatever reason, wasn't told about it so when they hit the ground the MQ-1 fired off the Zuni and it's aim was true enough. It was a real shit show by then and Network had one line:

The Zuni had damaged but not disabled a bot with the moniker Gorman and killed an Advisor and the bird and the human flying it. The bot Vasquez and its Advisor were unharmed (We later learned named after some ``Former Canadian Never American'' as they style themselves, screenwriter who had penned \textit{The Terminator(1984)} in some fever dream or some such they claimed and my team never cared for it, I swear to Jesus. We knew it was written by an American woman.) They let the mangled Gorman take point.

Red Hen had been kneeling in the guts and blood of this kid and was ignoring the fax sheet papier-mâché on her body all splashed with blood, soggy with gore. It just had this little girl's face pressed against its scan screen with its sensor head exposed. Holding her tightly by the small of her back, arching her. That's what Gorman's dispatched mini drone recorded at least. Red Hen wasn't thinking logically as far as we could tell but we had become transfixed. All of Red Hen's thought were instantly classified. There was no way, then or now, to tell what it was thinking. It did have enough forethought to slide the microSD into its slot. All us remote Advisors were in an uproar as that microSD contained new mission parameters and we knew that the MQ-1 was getting it all in one form or another. To our immense relief we heard the whistle and snap of automatic fire.

Gorman instantly became an American hero to us. We were getting the action from many angels and with Red Hen becoming such a talented cinematographer it made for amusing viewing. Gorman's aim wasn't true, however. The Zuni had damaged enough to make Red Hen aware just in time to counter and avoid much damage. Red Hen was able to grab its ``MAC-10'' and squeeze off a few rounds that struck Gorman's standard issue rifle and tore into him at the right spots and perhaps even weakened one of its sensor head's ``eyes''. We later discovered someone had stocked high velocity or ``HV'' ammo for the M10 in the crate. Logs indicate it was most likely accidental. Gorman's reaction happened fast: rushing Red Hen, grabbing him by the neck and shoving him against the cabin's wall to begin punching at the speed of anime directly at Red Hen's torso. Motivated by the slightest sight of any black as motor oil blood that ran through the microfiber tubes that were their veins. Red Hen began its own punching focused on Gorman's left face plate or eye. He got many good licks and dented the damn thing to the point he was able to wedge one of his upper right hand's fingers underneath the corrugated wound and with his upper arm's left hand drew his M9 and fired away at the innards of Gorman's ``skull''. 

Most of the vital workings of the bot aren't located there but enough matter and Gorman was damaged enough so that his backups weren't working as they should have been. It was at this point, as you know as well as I from the logs, that Red Hen stood up and the cameras were mostly offline so we didn't get a great glimpse. Vasquez and her Advisor were unloading everything heavy they had. Red Hen was undoubtedly being splintered apart with bullet hit squibs bursting. The footage from Vasquez did show him in a very Hollywood position of death: on his knees in a pool of his own bot guts and black engine oil. Almost Christlike? No! Thích Quảng Đức-like by God! The Advisor gathered up the microSD and faithfully crushed it.

Vasquez looked down at Red Hen and hung for a long time. The Advisor was scrambling for extract and was getting confused with each passing order not acknowledged. Vasquez looked up at a hole blown out of the cabin's ceiling from God knows what munition or perhaps it had just been torn off by all the rounds they had let off? She saw the MQ-1 and she saw the moon behind it and it was as if a lamp within glass stashed away in a nook suddenly became lit all within the reflection of her sensor head plate. She raised her sidearm and pointed it at her Advisor and her aim was true.\\
